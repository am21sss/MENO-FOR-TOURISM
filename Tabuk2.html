Tabuk - A seaside city filled with beauty

Tabuk region boasts its wonderful coastline, which extends about 700 kilometers north of the Red Sea, and boasts an enormous wealth of natural beauty that captivates its visitors and vacationers. The region will be further adorned with the most beautiful natural landscapes, especially after the launch of Saudi Vision 2030, which paid unprecedented attention to environmental elements and tourism development along the sea coast, which will be decorated with super development projects represented by NEOM City, The Line, Amala, and the Red Sea Project.

Tabuk region is adorned with about a hundred pristine islands that exude life away from modern life technologies and relax off the beaches in a unique diversity between a coral, sandy and volcanic environment. Its beaches also sparkle with soft white sand caressed by clear turquoise waters. Sand, water and climate harmonize in captivating natural scenes from the Haql Governorate in the north, through the Al-Bidaa and Duba Governorates, all the way to the Al-Wajh and Umluj Governorates in the southwest of the region.

Georgios G. Shipwreck

In Tabuk Governorate, specifically in Haql Governorate, the half sunken Georgios G ship is located in the clear waters of the beach and against a backdrop
of high mountains, forming a unique view and an amazing background. The sunken ship makes the landscape on this beach appear picturesque, with the Sinai
Mountains clearly visible on the other side of the Gulf of Aqaba.



Jabal Al-Lawz

Jabal al-Lawz in Tabuk is one of the highest mountain peaks in Saudi Arabia, reaching a height of about 2,600 meters above sea level. It is characterized
by its extreme cold, as it is the only mountain on which snow falls in the winter in Saudi Arabia. Many times this mountain is affected by incoming weather
depressions. From the Levant regions, accompanied by cold air masses, coming from the polar regions.

Maldives, and the most important of these islands is: Umm Sahar Island, an island. Umm Al-Malik, Atawil Island and others.
